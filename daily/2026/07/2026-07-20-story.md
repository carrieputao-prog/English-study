## 全英文版

A critical global situation abruptly emerged one sunny afternoon: a colossal, gleaming saucer hovered silently above New York. Its massive bay doors slowly began to release thousands upon thousands of giant yellow rubber chickens! No nation on Earth possessed an immediate alternative defense system against such a peculiar threat. The trajectory of these rubber chickens was surprisingly consistent, always landing with uncanny accuracy on the busiest city streets or most iconic landmarks, emitting ear-splitting squeaks. Top global think tanks struggled to attribute a profound meaning to this attack, some positing it was a cosmic prank from a higher civilization. Governments frantically tried to adjust their strategies; for instance, installing gigantic chicken-coop nets on city landmarks or covering them with trampolines. The impact of these soft but massive "weapons" was primarily psychological rather than physically destructive. Yet, the mental strain was considerable, as omnipresent squeaks and rubbery feathers filled the air globally. Moreover, the saucer projected a dazzling rainbow light show at night, blasting retro disco music. This left humanity utterly bewildered, wondering what exactly they were trying to convey. Perhaps they simply wished to inform us that the universe is far stranger than we imagined, and they really enjoy rubber toys.
