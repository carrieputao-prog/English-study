# 2026-07-09｜云的专属空间

## 本轮 10 个单词

## 中英穿插故事

城市议会决定为失踪的云朵allocate（分配，分派）专用空间。这是一个异常abstract（抽象的）概念，因为没有人能identify（认出，鉴定；确认）最近的云朵是否真的失踪了。有人提出疑问，此举是否对市容beneficial（有益的，有利的）。furthermore（此外，而且），他们需要确保这个空间对未来可能出现的云朵是accessible（易接近的，可进入的），或许是一片能漂浮着进去的平台。建造团队得想办法accommodate（容纳；提供住宿）云朵不断变化的形态，比如雷雨云和积雨云，这简直是工程学的噩梦。虽然没有任何确凿的evidence（证据，证明）表明云朵需要这样的设施，甚至云朵是否拥有知觉都难以判断，但市长认为这对于提升城市神秘感是essential（必要的，本质的）。however（然而），反对派指出，投资巨额资金去容纳一个虚无缥缈的目标显得极为荒谬。最终，他们建造了一个巨大的、透明的玻璃穹顶，里面充满了定制的湿度控制器和微风发生器，期待着某天能有一朵孤独的云来此安家，享受专属的宁静。

## 全英文版

The city council decided to allocate a dedicated space for missing clouds. This was an exceptionally abstract concept, as no one could identify if any recent clouds were actually lost. Questions arose as to whether this move would be beneficial to the city's image. Furthermore, they needed to ensure this space would be accessible to any future clouds that might appear, perhaps a floating platform they could drift into. The construction team had to figure out how to accommodate the clouds' constantly changing forms, such as thunderclouds and cumulonimbus clouds, which proved to be an engineering nightmare. While there was no concrete evidence that clouds needed such facilities, or even if clouds possessed sentience, the mayor deemed it essential for enhancing the city's sense of mystery. However, opposition pointed out that investing massive funds to accommodate such an ephemeral target seemed utterly ridiculous. Ultimately, they constructed a gigantic, transparent glass dome filled with custom humidity controllers and breeze generators, hoping that one day a solitary cloud would settle there to enjoy its exclusive tranquility.
