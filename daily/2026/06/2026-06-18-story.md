## 中英穿插故事

莉拉宇航员降落在一个由巨大彩色果冻组成的星球上，她的initial（最初的，开头的）目标是研究这独特的软体世界。很快，一股巨大的、脉动的果冻current（水流，电流）从地平线升起，径直朝她的着陆点涌来。莉拉试图identify（认出，鉴定；确认）这股流动的物质，它似乎有意识，并释放出友善的震动。这太匪夷所思了，然而，莉拉感觉到这股能量并非恶意。果冻流的友好意图是apparent（显然的，明显的），但它巨大的体积无疑会involve（包含；牵涉）吞没她的飞船。她必须立刻resolve（决定；解决（问题））如何与这奇异的生命形式互动。这股流并非完全accessible（易接近的，可进入的），因为它会突然凝固又突然液化，让物理接触变得困难。莉拉突然有了个insight（洞察力，见识）：这或许是星球向她打招呼的方式，虽然有点“粘人”。Nevertheless（然而），为了避免误会，莉拉决定播放一段宇宙通用问候语。与此同时（meanwhile（与此同时）），飞船的仪表板显示，星球目前的（当前的）地壳正在经历剧烈震颤，意味着她与这粘稠巨物的“交流”时间非常有限。

## 全英文版

Astronaut Lira landed on a planet made of enormous, colorful jelly, her initial mission being to study its unique gelatinous geology. The ground quivered like a colossal dessert with every step. Soon, a massive, pulsating current of jelly emerged from the horizon, flowing directly towards her landing craft. Lira attempted to identify the composition of this moving substance, only to find it appeared sentient, radiating friendly vibrations. This was utterly absurd; nevertheless, she sensed no malice from this flowing current. Its benevolent intentions were apparent, yet its sheer volume would undoubtedly involve engulfing her spaceship. She had to resolve immediately how to interact with this strange life form. The jelly current was not entirely accessible, as it would suddenly solidify then liquefy, making physical contact challenging. Lira suddenly gained an insight: perhaps this was the planet's way of greeting her, albeit a very "sticky" one. Meanwhile, the ship's current dashboard readings indicated severe tectonic tremors across the planet's crust, meaning her time for "interacting" with the gigantic, viscous entity was severely limited.
