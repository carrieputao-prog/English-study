# 2026-06-25｜色彩失窃案与磁铁奇谋

## 中英穿插故事

在一个曾经色彩斑斓的城市，彩虹盗贼突然降临，将所有鲜艳的色彩逐一窃取。市长面对这个完全 abstract（抽象的）的犯罪，感到困惑不解。however（然而），市民们失去了笑容，他们纷纷 claim（声称；索取）自己被偷走的蓝色天空和红色夕阳，对政府施加巨大压力。他深知，色彩是城市居民快乐的 essential（必要的，本质的）组成部分。这种无色状态对艺术家的创作自由和公众的情绪都产生了巨大的 negative impact（影响，作用；撞击）。市长发布紧急声明：otherwise（否则，不然），城市将永久陷入沉闷的灰暗。他与一群怪异的科学家制定了一项前所未有的 strategy（战略，策略）：用特殊颜料制造的巨大磁铁去吸回飘散在空气中的色彩粒子。这听起来很荒谬，但当时没有其他 sensible alternative（可供替代的 (n.) 替代品）了。色彩的回归被认为是 inevitable（不可避免的，必然的），然而，最初恢复的色彩只占了很小的 proportion（比例，部分），就像残缺的梦境。

## 全英文版

In a once vibrant city, the Rainbow Thief suddenly appeared, systematically stealing every vivid hue. The Mayor found this utterly abstract crime deeply perplexing. However, the citizens, stripped of their vibrant surroundings, lost their smiles. They began to claim their stolen blue skies and red sunsets, exerting immense pressure on the government. The Mayor knew that color was an essential component of the city residents' happiness. This colorless existence had a profound negative impact on artistic freedom and public morale. He issued an urgent declaration: otherwise, the city would forever descend into dull grayness. Working with a team of eccentric scientists, he devised an unprecedented strategy: to create gigantic magnets, made of special pigments, designed to attract the scattered color particles floating in the air. It sounded utterly ludicrous, but there seemed to be no other sensible alternative at the time. The return of color was considered inevitable, yet, initially, only a tiny proportion of hues reappeared, like fragments of a distorted dream. The abstract notion of stolen color now felt intensely real as people tried to recall the precise shades they had lost.
